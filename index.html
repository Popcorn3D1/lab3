What is plastic polution?

Plastic pollution is a pervasive environmental issue affecting our planet's ecosystems, wildlife, and human health. The widespread use of plastics, combined with inadequate waste management systems, has led to a massive accumulation of plastic waste in landfills, oceans, and natural environments.


Sources of Plastic Pollution

  • Single-use plastics (e.g., plastic bags, bottles, straws)
  • Packaging materials
  • Microplastics from the breakdown of larger plastics
  • Industrial waste
  • Fishing gear

Impacts of Plastic Pollution

  • Marine Life: Entanglement, ingestion, and habitat destruction.
  • Land Ecosystems: Soil contamination, harm to wildlife, and disruption of food chains.
  • Human Health: Potential exposure to harmful chemicals and microplastics.
  • Environmental Degradation: Visual pollution, litter, and damage to natural landscapes.

What Can Be Done?

  • Recycle properly.
  • Support businesses with sustainable practices.
  • Participate in clean-up efforts.
  • Advocate for policies that reduce plastic production and improve waste management.
